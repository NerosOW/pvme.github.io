    Basic Guide: Shadow Reef (Melee)

    Disclaimer: This guide is not made for beginners to the melee style. If experience with melee is limited, it is still possible to solo ED3, but please consider referring to #dpm-advice-melee first or YouTube beginner guides on soloing ED3 with melee.

    For the sake of summary, all boss attacks and mechanics are mentioned relative to melee rotation timings and details are omitted. For full mechanics of bosses, visit #the-shadow-reef-melee.

    Crassian Leviathan

    The first boss you will encounter is the Crassian Leviathan. Poison is extremely important for this boss to reduce its healing.

    Mechanics

    ⬥ Poison Pits: The center of the arena is filled with poison. Never stand here (not a concern with melee) or you will get stunned.

    ​

    ⬥ Dragon Breath: The Leviathan fires a prolonged breath attack in front of it, dealing rapid heavy typeless damage to those caught in it.

    ⬥ Head Sweep: The Leviathan do 2 head sweeps, dealing very heavy damage to anyone caught in the area.

    ⬥ After doing a Dragon Breath and 2 sets of Head Sweeps, the Leviathan will move to other side of the arena, repeating his previous rotation.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Flanking can be used on the Crassian Leviathan on this side.

    Strategy

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• You do not need to click if using a halberd type weapon.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Stop clicking when using a channel, to avoid cancelling. The channel itself will keep you in place.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Click while cancelling channels with a non-channeled ability to prevent movement.

    Taraket the Necromancer

    The second boss is Taraket which attacks using primarily damaging but slow Magic autos. You will need good DPS to avoid getting overwhelmed at this boss. is thus almost mandatory as it provides 20% additional damage and accuracy to Taraket and his minions.

    Mechanics

    ⬥ Rift of Undeath: Taraket spawns a rift with 30k HP that occasionally spawns skeletons. After 30 seconds, it explodes dealing heavy typeless damage. This is prevented by killing it.

    ⬥ Death's Swiftness: Taraket shrouds himself in , becoming immobile and switches from using Mage to Ranged, starting to hit harder.

    ⬥ Corpse Carrier and Bloated Monstrosity: At 200K HP, Taraket will become invulnerable and fly to either black crystal at the sides of the arena, healing for 100k HP and spawning a minion: Corpse Carrier (west) or the Bloated Monstrosity (east), with 90K HP each.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• The Corpse Carrier will continuously spawn skeletons.

    ​ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• The Bloated Monstrosity increases the damage of Taraket's attacks.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Bloat will spawn first by default.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Taraket will not spawn anymore minions after spawning both.

    ⬥ Spirit Line: While in after spawning both minions, Taraket will speak dialogue and smash the ground infront of him for heavy melee damage, summoning a line of spirits infront of him that moves towards the player, dealing rapid typeless damage upon contact.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Flanking is applicable if the player moves behind Taraket during this brief period.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If you Taraket right as he uses this attack, Taraket will turn around and you will completely avoid both attacks.

    Strategy

    ⬥ Use as much as possible as there are no accuracy issues.

    ⬥ To start the fight, execute a rotation on Taraket.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If spawning Carrier first, move Taraket west after , such as using while standing east of Taraket.

    ⬥ flicking is extremely effective for Taraket as his attacks are slow and telegraphed.

    ⬥ Around the time ends, Taraket will spawn a Rift of Undeath.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If Taraket still has >30k HP from 200K, perform the following abilities to phase him and kill the portal in time:

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- followed by mid gcd. Walk back until 2 tiles from the south wall (lure demonstrated in gif below).

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Use 2 defensives after .

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- back and bleed a Threshold on the portal.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Use AoE abilities targeting the portal to kill it and help phase Taraket to below 200k HP. Clear the skeletons spawned halberd with resets.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If Taraket is near 200k when portal spawns, simply phase Taraket and use and powerful thresholds to clear the portal.

    ⬥If Taraket is flying and there is nothing to kill, use defensives or basics on Taraket to generate adrenaline.

    ⬥ What you want him spawning first depends on your level of skill as a meleer:

    The Ambassador

    The final boss of Elite Dungeon 3 is The Ambassador (Ambi), a highly damaging boss that attacks with 100% accuracy. The boss primarily attacks with ranged , with a heavy mage attack after every 5 autos. The mage hit is replaced by a small but instant melee swipe if the player is in melee distance. flicking is heavily recommended, else a secondary preset with more food is likely needed just for Ambi.

    Mechanics

    ⬥ Ambi's attack rotation from 1M-550K HP (Phase 1):

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Mage/Melee auto → 5 autos → Purple Corruption → 5 autos ‎ ‎→ Black Hole → 5 autos ‎ ‎→ Purple Corruption → 5 autos ‎ ‎→ Sinister Fragments → Repeat Magic Auto-Attack

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Purple Corruption: Ambi fires a spout into the player, dealing no damage and placing a bar over their head.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- When this bar nears depletion, a 5x5 area around the player will become corrupted until Seiryu clears them.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- 2 Corruptions are dropped when the bar depletes, separated by 2 ticks.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- These zones ramp up from doing 200 to 2000 typeless damage periodically to anyone standing inside.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Black Hole: Ambi will spawn a black hole which explodes a few seconds afterwards, dealing 8000+ damage to the player.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Stunning the blackhole negates the attack.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Sinister Fragments: At 650k or at 59 seconds, six fragments will disperse from the boss to the edges of the arena with 20K HP each. Ambi will also fire three slowly rotating beams that last for about a minute.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Ambi absorbs any alive fragments after a minute, releasing a hard hitting typeless explosion per fragment absorbed.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- The beams always spawn in set locations (N, SE, SW), and rotate counterclockwise slowly.

    ⬥ 550K-400K (Phase 2): Ambi mostly follows the same auto attack pattern from P1, but stops spawning Sinister Fragments.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Ambi will periodically spawn 2 invulnerable Crassian Ritual Keepers.

    ⬥ 400K-0 (Phase 3):

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Ambi will stop doing Purple Corruption and Black Hole, continuing his auto attack pattern as normal.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Seiryu will emerge shortly after 400K, clearing all Crassians and Purple Corruption.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• A ring of black hands will spawn to encompass the edge of the arena.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- The hands will slam down in a clockwise fashion, sending constant smoke projectiles towards the Ambi.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- These will heal Ambi 2500 HP each upon contact. They can be blocked, directing a small amount of damage towards the player.

    ⬥ Magic Onslaught: Upon spawning the black hands, every 10 auto attacks, the Ambassador will send out 5 extremely hard hitting Magic attacks.

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• ‎ ‎This is signalled by Ambi saying "Fall now and be forgotten", followed by opening his arms.
