    Introduction

    ⬥ In RuneScape PvM, the meta at the time of writing is about pushing out as much damage in as little time as possible. It is important to understand that good and efficient PvM, especially at the higher tiers, is about squeezing out as much damage as possible in between mechanics. This channel will go over general damage output lessons, ability prioritizations, crafting a rotation, a learning path, common mistakes, and tips and tricks.

    General Background Knowledge

    ⬥ For general PvM terminology, DPM tips, and revolution bars, see #dpm-advice-faq

    Ability Prioritizations & Building a Rotation

    Ability Prioritization

    ⬥ Generally speaking, high damaging abilities should be prioritized and used more often than low damaging abilities

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• However, there's a catch: high damaging abilities often come with long cooldowns

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- If you use all of the high damaging abilities at once, you will be forced to wait for cooldowns before you may use them again. During this time you are forced to use weak abilities

    ⬥ To avoid this problem, the trick is to use filler abilities (ones with decent damage and short cooldowns) in-between your high damaging abilities in order to pace your cooldowns

    Building a Rotation

    ⬥ When building a rotation, ability prioritizations and cooldowns should be on your mind

    ⬥ You generally will want to prioritize building up to 100% adrenaline and activating Sunshine as soon as possible

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Ideally, enter the encounter with 100% adrenaline so you may activate it immediately upon entry

    ⬥ Both within and outside of Sunshine , you will want to prioritize your strong thresholds, special attacks, and modified ultimates. These include Igneous Omnipower , Wild Magic , Asphyxiate , flanking Deep Impact , and Guthix Staff

    ⬥ Greater Concentrated Blast should form the foundation of your rotation. It is a very powerful basic ability with a short cooldown and provides a critical strike chance buff on a subsequent ability

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Use often as a filler ability, in-between other strong basics (Wrack and Ruin , Dragon Breath , Corruption Blast , flanking Impact , and walked Combust ) and the aforementioned thresholds, modified ultimates, and special attacks

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• A basic pattern of → Ability → Ability → repeat is recommended

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If not yet unlocked, or if camping 2H, can be substituted with Concentrated Blast (2-hit) or Sonic Wave , respectively

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• The critical chance buff gained through / is wasted if the follow-up ability is a bleed ( / )

    ⬥ Any basic ability with damage lower than Sonic Wave or Concentrated Blast is considered a weak ability (Wrack , Tuska's Wrath , Sacrifice , non-flank Impact , Chain , unwalked Combust )

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• These should be largely avoided

    ⬥ Juggling both DW and 2H weapons gives you access to both (Greater) Concentrated Blast / , and Sonic Wave as filler abilities

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• This may provide you with more breathing room while improvising rotations

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Care should be taken while weapon juggling so as not to delay usage of if unlocked. In this case, should only be used as a fallback to take the place of a weaker ability

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• If is not yet unlocked, a general pattern of → Ability → → Ability → repeat is recommended

    ⬥ Generally, if using Exsanguinate , it is recommended to use Wrack and Ruin if the alternative is weaker than Dragon Breath

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• This means Blood Tithe stacks can be retained to boost strong basics such as or , while saving for a future time where a weak ability would have to be used otherwise

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Not affected by Precise or Equilibrium

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Hits every 2t (1.2s), for a total of 5 times

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• It is Walkable:

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Doubles damage of each hit

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• It is affected by Lunging :

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Max increases by 20% per Lunging rank

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Walking bleed only causes it to increase by 50% damage

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• It deals two additional hits if used within 10 seconds after Wrack and Ruin

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• It is affected by modified by Kerapac's wrist wraps :

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- The next to hit within 10t of the cast will have its remaining hits instantly apply at 25% more damage

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Within 10t meaning if is cast t0, has to hit within t0-9, meaning latest you can cast is t7

    4TAA

    What it is

    ⬥ 4-tick Auto Attack (4TAA), is an extension of weapon juggling wherein, as the name implies, you force an auto attack on the 4th tick after casting the ability before

    ⬥ As mentioned earlier, when you cast a damaging ability it will put your autos on cooldown

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• 4t cooldown after a fastest speed (wand) ability, 6t after an average speed (staff) ability

    ⬥ So to 4TAA, we make use of this fact, along with the fact that Magic can force an auto + ability on the same tick due to having access to spell (and thus auto) binds

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• We make use of this by delaying our next ability by a tick to force an auto and ability on the 4th tick

    How it Works

    An Overview of FSOA Usage

    ⬥ The Staff of Armadyl's immensely powerful special attack, Instability, consists of two components:

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• An initial hit of 50%-200% ability damage

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Firing an auto-attack when a critical hit is landed

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- The auto casts the tick the hit lands

    ⬥ Auto-attacks released by can themselves crit. In such cases, auto-attacks will be fired recursively

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Recursively-fired auto-attacks will only occur if the critical hit lands on the primary target

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- Crits on additional targets (such as from ) will not trigger the effect

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• The damage of fired auto-attacks is determined by the equipped weapon when fired

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎- For this reason, a 2H weapon should be preferred over dual-wields

    ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎• Fired auto-attacks will not interrupt channelled abilities; however, they will incur auto-attack cooldown

    ⬥ Due to Instability being focused around critical hits, it has excellent synergy with Tsunami for adrenaline generation from both abilities and subsequent auto-attacks

    ⬥ The special attack costs 50% adrenaline, lasts for 30 seconds, and has a cooldown of 60 seconds

    Key Considerations

    ⬥ An effective Staff of Armadyl rotation is made possible through landing critical hits with 2H equipped as often, and reliably as possible
